Intro to management

[TOC]

Definition of management

As a universal human activity

Management is the activity of getting things done with the aid of people and other resources.

  • WHAT
  • when people take responsibility for an activity
  • HOW
  • “Getting things done with the aid of people and other resources”
  • By planning, organizing, leading and controlling
  • ‘Management’ and ‘work’ combined

Types of manager

Functional Specialization

General mangers

  • are responsible for the performance of a distinct unit of the organization
  • rely on managers in charge of each function
  • e.g. distinct units: division, subsdiary

Functional managers

  • line

    are responsible for the performance of activities that directly meet customers’ needs

  • staff

    are responsible for the performance of activities that support lien managers

  • project

    are responsible for managing a project, usually intended to change some element of an organization or its context

  • entrepreneur

    are people who see opportunities in a market, and quickly mobilize the resources to deliver the product or service profitably

Hierarchies

  • people doing the work

    • include both manual and mental work

    • from low-paid cleaners to Apple software designers

  • First-line managers—supervising people doing the work

    • direct and support the daily work of a group of staff
    • monitor, report, improve work performance

  • middle managers- managing supervisors

    • ensure that first-line managers work in line with company policies

    • interpret and reshape higher policies to suit local conditions

      translate strategy into operational tasks

  • senior managers-managing middle managers

    • ensure the middle managers, suppliers and other business partners work in ways that add value to resources

    P.S. contain some external elements

  • board of directors- manage the business

    • be aware of the work of senior managers, but spend most of its time looking into the future or dealing with external affairs

Manager—add value by influencing others

Someone who gets things done with the aid of people and other resources

Mintzberg’s10 roles

Informational

  • monitor

  • disseminator(传播者)

    Forward information to others, send memos, make phone calls

    e.g. share content of applications with team members by email

  • spokesperson

Interpersonal

  • figurehead(挂名首脑)

    perform ceremonial and symbolic duties, receive visitors

    e.g. sign letters of award to successful applications

  • leader

  • liaison（联络人）

    Maintain information links in and beyond the organizations

Decisional

  • entrepreneur

  • disturbance handler

    take corrective actions during crisis, resolve conflicts amongst staff, adapt to challenges

    e.g. handing staff grievances

  • resource allocator

  • negotiator

    represent unit during negotiations with unions,suppliers, and generally defend interests

Managers as networks

  • behaviors that aim to build, maintain and use informal relationships that may help
    work-related activities.
  • is good for your career (but not always enough to be effective)

Management tasks

  • Planning – overall direction of work

  • Organizing – allocating resources, time and effort

  • Leading – generating effort and commitment towards objectives

  • Controlling – monitor progress, compare with plan, adjust if needed

Apply to all kinds of tasks and done iteratively

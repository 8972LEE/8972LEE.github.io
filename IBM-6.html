  • International joint ventures

PESTEL& Social-Cultural culture context

  • ¨Many pay particular attention to socio-cultural factors, and how they differ between
    nations.

Meaning of culture

Context and culture

  • High-context

    meaning depends on shared experience and understanding

    e.g. Japan, Arab countries, Southern Europe

  • low-context

    meaning is clear and explicit

    e.g. US, Germany, Scandinavia

Attitude to conflict and harmony

  • Some countries see dissent as normal and healthy, and expect conflicts (The Netherlands)
  • ¤Others value harmony, especially that junior staff accept views of seniors (East Asia)

Attitude to change

  • Others value harmony, especially that junior staff accept views of seniors (East Asia)

Attitude to time

  • An infinite resource or a scarce resource to manage?

Hofstede’s theory of national cultures

Power distance

degree to which less powerful members of organizations accept the fact that power is not distributed equally

  • High willingness to accept variations in power

    e.g France and Brazail

  • Low willingness to accept variation in power

    e.g UK and Sweden (瑞典)

Individualism /collectivism

  • Individual societies stress individual initiative and responsibility

    –e.g. US and UK

  • Collectivist societies stress loyalty to group in return for support

    -e.g. South America and Asia

Uncertainty avoidance

The extent to which people feel threatened by ambiguous situations

  • Countries with high UA scores are less willing to tolerate ambiguity

    e.g. Japan, Korea, Latin America

  • Countries with low UA scores are more willing to tolerate ambiguity

    e.g. China and most Asian countries, US, UK,

Masculinity && Femininity

Masculinity : Dominant values of a society are success, money and material goods

  • show preference for assertive behaviour

    • This society will be driven by competition, achievement and success, with success defined by the winner/best in the field— a value system that starts in school and continues throughout the organizational life.

    e.g. Japan

Femininity: Dominant values of a society are caring for others and the quality of life

Long-term/ short-term orientation

describes how every society has to maintain some links with its own past while dealing with the challenges of the present and future.

  • long-term orientated societies value rewards that will come far into the future

    e.g Japan and China

  • short-term orientated societies value the past and the present, a respect for tradition

    e.g UK, US Canada

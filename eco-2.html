经济学人||外媒谈“二人转”的危机

Posted on 2019-03-30 | In the economist | | Visitors: |
Words count in article: | Reading time ≈

：），最近在复习GMAT考试，愈发感悟逻辑与架构的重要性，遂忆起经济学人这位大佬，准备一周两篇精读文章与大伙们分享呀。

这篇文章摘自《经济学人》2019年3月16日中国专栏。文章主题是传统文化“二人转”，但此篇文章一反对于传统文化赞扬推崇的普遍观点，深刻指出了“二人转”存在的糟粕，及其面临的实际发展困境。句意简洁，且充分结合中国实际。此处，我 们着重研究分析其中两段——（以“赵本山”为切入点剖析传统“二人转”面临的挑战与转型必要性）

整体

标题

Comedy: A blind eye to turn

  • “喜剧：睁一只眼闭一只眼”
  • 来源：Turning a blind eye is an idiom describing the ignoring of undesirable information.
  • 暗示二人转存在乱象，但当局态度不甚明朗。

背景

  • 二人转（two-person-turn）
    • 来自经济学人的注释：A traditional form of comic song-and dance that often involves raunchy gags.
    • raunchy gags : 粗俗（coarse）的笑话（jokes）

局部

结构

President Xi Jinping is no fan of lewd comedy.

  • 鲜明表达出X的态度——”is no fan of“

In 2014, in a speech on the role of the arts, Mr Xi said some artists were spewing
out “cultural garbage”. He demanded that creative works serve the Communist
Party and not “provoke the ecstasy of the senses”.

  • 此小节援引X关于艺术的公开讲话作为例证支持上方观点。

It may be no coincidence that Mr Zhao has not appeared on national television’s
Chinese New Year gala since Mr Xi assumed power in 2012.

  • 描述赵本山（作为二人转代表）受中央政策层面的负面影响，由中央宏观到文化微观。

He had once been a regular (in cleaned-up form) on the hugely popular show.

Last year Mr Zhao was booted out of the advisory body to China’s**

parliament. His flamboyant** lifestyle mayalso have contributed to his

fall from grace.

  • 为上小节赵所受负面影响提供证据，同时为下文招的应对作铺垫。

Mr Zhao is now leading a campaign to bowdlerize errenzhuan.

  • 本段主题：赵的改革与应对

In his chain of theatres he puts on only family fare. Dirty jokes and swearing are all but banned. Other
venues have followed suit. Television stations only invite the cleanest errenzhuan performers. Ms He, the head teacher, says she does not teach her students any dirty lines or gestures. A Changchun resident says this trend may explain why attendance at errenzhuan theatres is falling.

  • 列举 限制戏院表演内容，电视节目严要求，课堂改革，作为赵转型应对政策的实例，同时指出隐患： ”二人转“观众减少的趋势

表达

  • is no fan of:

    很形象的表达反对意见，比之 disagree，object，refute，denounce，disprove 之流更为生动。

  • spew out

    • 此处spew意味吐出 【to vomit (= bring food from the stomach back out through the mouth) 】
    • “sp” 作为前缀，常表示分散，分开之意，如spend，spout（喷出），spur [spə:] n刺激，马刺 ，spawn 产卵。
    • “spew out culture garbage” 形象描绘出“文化垃圾”的恶心呕吐过程

  • demand：一直被忽视的动词用法

    • to ask for sth very firmly 强烈要求

  • provoke the ecstasy of the
    senses：

    • provoke ： to cause a particular reaction or have a particular effect 激起；
      • pro（在前面）+voke（voc–声音）冲着人面前喊–激起
    • ecstasy ：a feeling or state of very great happiness 狂喜；

  • It may be no coincidence that ：

    • 很好的顺承句式，表明下句与上句关系匪浅

      e.g This also suggests（暗示） that

      ​ This is also an evidence for

  • was booted out of the advisory body to China’s
    parliament

    • boot ： 作名词是靴子，做动词表示踢，解雇

    • advisory body 表示 顾问团

      • advisory：having the role of giving professional advice 顾问的，咨询的

    • parliament：议会

      e.g. to be elected to Parliament 当选为议会议员

  • flamboyant ：

    • different, confident and exciting in a way that attracts attention 炫耀的；卖弄的

    • flame（火）+boy+ant（adj）像火一样的男孩爱炫耀

  • bowdlerize

    • 删改
    • 词源： Bowdler 删改了莎士比亚原著

  • Other venues have followed suit.

    • venues: places 场所

    • follow suit ：跟着做

      e.g. They focused on signing up merchants for the program with the promise that the affiliates would follow suit.

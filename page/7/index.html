How to deal with difficult sentences?

Posted on 2019-03-27 | In gmat | | Visitors:
Words count in article: | Reading time ≈

Main classification

复杂修饰成分

  • 从句（主语，宾语，定语，状语，同位语）
  • 介词短语修饰
  • 分词修饰
  • 不定式修饰

  • 定语从句

    e.g. The methods that a company devices to perpetuate itself come into being to preserve aspects of the cultural legacy that that community perceives as essential

​ Strategy: 先找主谓宾（主语为：The methods，谓语为 come into being 而后是一个以不定式to 引导表示目的的状语）

  • 主语从句

    e.g.That each large firm will act with consideration of its own needs and thus avoid selling its products for more than its competitors charge is commonly recognized by advocates of free-market economic theories

​ Strategy: 句首出现了以that 引导的长从句为主语

​ 把长从句理解成一个名词作主语，中文翻译时，句尾加上“这一点”

  • 宾语从句

    e.g. The historian Frederick wrote in the 1890’s that the agrarian discontent that had been developing steadily in the united states since about 1870 had been precepted by the closing of the internal frontier — that is , the depletion of available new land needed for further expansion of the American farming system.

​ Strategy: 注意that 按顺序读即可

  • 长状语

    e.g. The appreciation of traditional oral American Indian literature has been limited, hampered by poor translations and by the difficulty, even in the rare culturally sensitive and aesthetically satisfying translation, of completely conveying the original’s verse structure, tone, and syntax.

​

​ Strategy: 状语部分分割了结构 the difficulty of 的结构

​ 状语能表示时间地点方式程度原因结果背景，且出现在句子任意位置

  • 层层修饰

    e.g. A very specialized feeding adaptation in zooplankton is that of the tadpole like appendicular Ian who lives in a walnut-sized or smaller balloon of mucus equipped with filters that capture and concentrate phytoplankton

​ Strategy: 从who 开始对宾语的三层修饰

​

  • 并列成分

    e.g They were fighting, albeit discreetly, to open the intellectual world to the new science and to liberate intellectual life from ecclesiastical philosophy and they envisioned their work as contributing to the growth , not of philosophy but of research in mathematics and physics

​ Strategy:第一个and连接两个并列的不定式，第二个and 连接两个完整的句子

  • 多重否定

大段插入语及同位语

  • 普通插入

    e.g. Moreover, I can feel strong emotions in response to objections of art that are interpretations, rather than representations, of reality.

​ Strategy: in response to 引导的，修饰emotion 的介词结构中，介词宾语被由that引导的定语从句所修饰，而定语从句中的表语被插入语 rather than representation 所分割

​ 删除成对逗号，再反读句意。

  • 短语被分割

    e.g. Such variation in size, shape, chemistry, conduction speed, excitation threshold, and the like as had been demonstrated in nerve cells remained negligible in significance for any possible correlation with the manifold dimensions of mental experience

    e.g. under the force of this view, it was perhaps inevitable that the art of rhetoric should pass from the status of being regarded as of questionable worth (because although it might be both a source of pleasure and a means to urge people to right action , it might also be a means to distort truth and a source of misguided action) to the status of being wholly condemned.

​ Strategy: such as , so that, too…to, more than. From A to B, between A and B

为避免头重脚轻而倒转至句末

  • e.g. The sex ratio will be favored which maximize the number of descendants an individual will have and hence the number of gene copies transmitted.

​ Strategy: 由于主语 the sex ratio 之后的以which引导的定语从句过长，原文将长长的定语从句倒装于谓语will be favoured 之前，避免头重脚轻之感

  • e.g. Black fiction surveys a wide variety of novels, bringing to our attention In the process some fascinating and little-known works like James Weldon Johnson’s Autobiography of an Ex–Colored man.

​ Strategy: bring 后接的长宾语被放置到句末

  • 及物动词加介词

    e.g. Yet Walzer’s argument, however deficient, does point to one of the most serious weakness of capitalism—–namely, that it brings to predominant positions in a society people who, no matter how legitimately they have earned their material rewards, often lack those other qualities that evoke affection or admiration

​ Strategy: throw over, insert into, import into, infer from, establish for, advocate as 常易被倒装

​ bring A to B ——- bring B A

  • 及物动词加副词

    e.g. Frank, however, predicted that women would be liberated from the ‘social, legal, and economic subordination ‘ of the family by technological developments that made possible the recruitment of the whole female into public industry.

​ Strategy: possible 被倒装到了前面

省略

e.g. The sex ratio will be favored which maximize the number of descendants（that） an individual will have and hence(maximizes) the number of gene copies transmitted.

Strategy: 在maximize 的第一个宾语 the number of descendants an individual will have 中，an individual will have是修饰descendants的定语从句，故省略引导词that.

​ 第二个 the number of 之前省略了同之前一样的maximize

  • 重复的成分

    e.g. The correlation of carbon dioxide with temperature, of course, does not establish whether changes in atmospheric composition caused the warming and cooling trends or were caused by them

Strategy: them 取代了 the warming and cooling trends

  • 让步转折的省略e.g.

Strategy: 让步和转折的词汇，同一个句子只能出现一次

​ 阅读时，有意识地补全让步和转折的语气，避免句子奇长

  • 定语从句引导词的省略

    e.g. Because the potential hazards(that) pollen grans are subject to as they are transported over long distance //are enormous,……..

Strategy:修饰主语 potential hazards 的定语从句引导词that被省略了

  • 定语从句中的引导词与系动词同时省略，变成后置定语

    e.g. qualities such as ‘the capacity for hard work ‘ essential in producing wealth

Strategy: 原：名词+定语从句引导词+形容词+介词结构

​ 现： 名词+形容词+介词结构

​ 因有介词结构，连带形容词一起后置

The Chian -US trade dispute ---- An ocean apart

Posted on 2019-03-27 | In the economist | | Visitors:
Words count in article: | Reading time ≈

Although hopes of a deal buoy up stock markets, a chasm remains

Background information

Last year, when American officials visited Beijing for trade negotiations, they spent more time fighting among themselves than against China. They could not agree on who should lead the talks or what their goal should be. Seeing such amateurism, their Chinese interlocutors reckoned that they had little to worry about.

  • main function: demonstrate the internal conflicts in America of previous negotiation between America and China. ( May be ready for a comparison)
  • Vocabulary：
    • amateurism ：amateur(业余的) + ism [as a hobby rather than for vocation]
    • interlocutors : inter+locut–loqui(to talk)+or[a middleman who speak on behalf of an organization]
    • reckon: guess or think

Many of the same Americans have been back in Beijing for more talks in recent days. But this time they had an undisputed leader—Robert Lighthizer, the hard-nosed United States Trade Representative—and a clear set of demands. Their Chinese counterparts, having seen President Donald Trump’s zeal for tariffs, knew that they had something to worry about after all.

  • main function : reveal the current circumstances between America and China

  • V&S:

    • undisputed：something is widely accepted as being true. To dispute is to argue, and when you add the prefix un-, “not,” you get something that can’t be argued with.

    • hard-nosed: being tough, stubborn, or uncompromising

    • zeal: eagerness and ardent interest in pursuit of something

America has set a deadline of March 1st for an agreement. If it is missed, tariffs on $200bn-worth of imports from China are due to rise from 10% to 25%, inflicting more pain on a slowing Chinese economy. That would invite a sharper backlash from China. Its ability to direct firms to shift purchases to other countries has already hurt American exporters (see chart). Chinese officials resent the deadline but it has focused minds. The latest talks, which began on February 11th and were due to end on February 15th, are the third round this year.

  • main function:
  • V&S: the third around negotiation because of their rivalry to each other
    • inflict : to cause (something unpleasant) to be endured
    • invite: to increase the likelihood of e.g. invite trouble
    • backlash: a strong adverse reaction (as to a recent political or social development)
    • focus minds : be absorbed in

All this has fed expectations that the two sides could soon make peace—or at least extend their truce. Mr Trump told reporters that he might let the deadline slide if a good deal is within reach. Another meeting between Mr Trump and Xi Jinping, China’s president, is under discussion. Investors have taken heart. American and Chinese stock markets have both risen by about 10% this year.

  • main function: two sides could make peace
  • V&S :
    • two sides could make peace
    • truce:a respite especially from a disagreeable or painful state or action
    • let the deadline slide: extend（defer ，adjourn） the deadline
    • take heart: gain courage

The outlines of a deal have been in view for a while. China would probably promise to buy lots of goods from America, from soya beans to natural gas, and allow foreign companies more access to its economy. America would cut tariffs and perhaps promise to remain open to Chinese investors, as long as they are not part of a state-backed assault on sensitive technology.

  • main function: the imagination of specific contents of outlines
  • V$S:
    • have been in view for a while
    • a state-backed assault on sensitive technology

Yet when negotiators get into the details, problems surface. After many frustrating years waiting for China to open its markets, the Americans suspect that its pledges will be empty. The Chinese suspect that America is motivated by a desire not for fair trade but for thwarting a new rival.

  • main function: they are skeptical to each other
  • V&S
    • pledge: promises
    • thwart: hinder or prevent (the efforts, plans, or desires) of

Two key outstanding questions are thus how to measure whether China lives up to its word, and what America can do if it fails. Scott Kennedy of the Centre for Strategic and International Studies in Washington says changes should be measured by China’s economic outcomes, not by its stated policies. The government has, for instance, agreed to scrap rules that foreign firms must find local partners to make cars in China. The test, says Mr Kennedy, ought to be whether foreign carmakers actually do set up wholly owned firms in China and operate them successfully.

  • main function: two measurements of the realization degree of Chinese promises
  • V&S:
    • live up to its words : fulfill their pledges
    • scrap : dispose of (something useless or old)

And if it is judged that China is not keeping its promises? One option is to submit disputes to neutral arbitration. Mr Lighthizer is said to dislike this idea. Another is to give America the right to slap tariffs unilaterally on Chinese goods—which China is understandably loth to accept. Even as the trade war seems to be cooling, a chasm still lies between the combatants.

  • main function: identify a chasm still lies between combatants , especially when China does not live up to its promises.
  • V&S:
    • disputes: controversies
    • arbitration: The person in charge of the arbitration acts like an official referee, helping both sides come to an agreement.
    • slap tariffs: levy customs
    • unilaterally: Doing something unilaterally means it’s done without the agreement or participation of other people it might affect.
    • loth: unwilling
    • chasm: A chasm is a deep divide, either literal or figurative, such as a giant chasm in an ice cap or the growing chasm between two friends who haven’t spoken in a long time
    • combatant: Someone who fights, especially in a war, is a combatant. Sometimes entire countries are described as combatants during military conflicts.

尽管达成协议的希望推高了股市，但分歧依然深刻,去年，美国官员到访北京进行贸易谈判时，他们花在内部争论上的时间比和中国官员谈判还要多。他们无法就谁应该领导谈判和谈判的目标是什么达成一致。看到这样业余的表现，他们的中国谈判对手认为自己没有什么好担忧的。

近日，这些美国官员中的很多人重返北京，展开更多磋商。但这一次他们有了一位无可争议的领导——固执的美国贸易代表罗伯特·莱特希泽（Robert Lighthizer），也有了一系列清晰的诉求。他们的中国谈判对手见识到了特朗普对关税的狂热，明白自己终究还是要有所担忧。

美国把 3 月 1 日定为达成协议的最后期限。如果不能如期达成，便将把价值 2000 亿美元的中国商品的关税从 10% 提升至 25%，这会让已经放缓的中国经济雪上加霜。而这将促使中国发起更激烈的反击。它可以直接指挥企业转向其他国家采购，美国的出口商已经因此受损（见图表）。中国官员对这一期限心怀不满，但明确的期限也让双方集中了注意力。最近一次谈判从 2 月 11 日持续到 15 日，是今年的第三轮谈判。

所有这些都让人预期双方可能很快会达成和解，或者至少会延长休战期。特朗普对记者说，如果令人满意的协议近在咫尺，他可能不会死盯着最后期限。双方正在讨论特朗普和习近平再进行一次会谈。投资者已受到鼓舞。今年美国和中国股市都上涨了 10% 左右。

协议生成雏形已有一段时日。中国可能将承诺购买从大豆到天然气的大量美国产品，并允许外国公司更多地参与本国经济。美国将削减关税，并可能承诺对中国投资者保持开放，只要他们不会在政府的支持下对敏感技术出手。

但当谈判双方深入细节时，问题就会出现。经过多年空等中国开放市场，美方怀疑中国的承诺只是空话。中方则怀疑美国的目的并非公平贸易，而是要打压一个新对手。

因此，两个有待解决的关键问题就是如何衡量中国是否兑现了承诺，以及若未兑现美国能怎么做。华盛顿战略与国际研究中心的斯科特·肯尼迪（Scott Kennedy）说，应该用中国的经济成果而不是它提出了怎样的政策来衡量改变。例如，中国政府已经同意废除外国公司必须和本地企业合资才能在中国制造汽车的规定。那么，肯尼迪说，衡量方法就是看外国汽车公司是否确实在中国建立了全资公司并成功经营。

而如果认定中国没有遵守承诺呢？一种方法是将争端提交中立机构仲裁。据说莱特希泽不喜欢这种做法。另一个方法是让美国有权单方面对中国商品征收关税——中国自然不乐意接受。就在贸易战看似降温之际，交战双方之间仍然横亘着一道鸿沟。

Good morning, everyone. I am Vanora Lee, a first-year student in NUBS here at UNNC. The topic of my AOP research focuses on methods for car-sharing start-ups to reduce cash flow pressure. I’ve divided today’s talk into 3 parts. First, I’ll introduce some background information about car-sharing in China. Then I’ll move on to two of the sources I’ve already found for using in my final presentation, including general ideas and choosing reasons. At the end, I am going to show my next plan for my final presentation.

So let’s begin. Do you notice this number in red? Do you know what it represents? Well, it’s the annual growth is expected for the Chinese car-sharing fleet until 2025, according to (Roland berger,2017). Although It seems like this emerging service is booming, the big danger is below the surface. Recently, many car-sharing companies went bankrupt because of cashflow pressure, such as Togo, Ezzy, Magua and so on. There is not doubt that the car-sharing as an environmentally friendly way of traveling contributes to the future sustainable development. Not only the Chinese government, but also consumers are looking forward to its wide application. So, it is definitely significant for us to consider how to reduce the cash flow pressure faced by car-sharing start-ups. That’s what my presentation will discuss.

Ok，let’s move on to the first resource I’ve found in Nu search. This source clearly analyzes the urban car sharing environment in China, based on their accurate research. In this article, the latent dangers of cash flow shortage confronted by most of car-sharing start-ups are precisely demonstrated, which gives me a comprehensive understanding of this topic. Additionally, this study published in 2017 is quite recent. Also, as you can see that, it’s from a peer-reviewed journal—-‘’Advances in Transportation Studies”, which means this article is relatively reputable and reliable. In conclusion, this source is quite academic and recent. I plan to use this source in the problem analyzation since it distinctly shows the current car-sharing circumstances in China and indicates cash flow pressure as the main obstacle of those companies.

Right, the second source I will be showing you today is a corporate report by Roland Berger company in 2017. This is their analysis of strategies for car-sharing start-ups to operate a successful business in China. Although it points out its popularity in Chinese market, it emphasizes that this industry is still in the initial stages. More importantly, it draws up creative and practical solutions aiming at specific problems, which inspires me a lot. Why did I choose this source? Well, initially, it is recent, and this is significant for this emerging industry’ s study. Besides, it’s reliable— Roland Berger, founded in 1967, is one of the leading global consultancies. This source is appropriate to be used in my solution part. I will be using these important central and local government policies of supporting car-sharing companies as evidence to show the feasibility of relying on assistances from the government.

Now, the final part for next plan. I’ve explained to you the purpose of my presentation, showed you two of the sources I intend using, and explained why I chose them. Everything looks good since so far. But there are still some aspects needed to be improved. In the future, I plan to find visual graphs and statistics about cash flow pressure, showing the magnitude of the problem more explicitly. As for solution, some specific successful examples should be added so that this solution could be more practical. Well, I think that covers most of what I want to say today. And here is my references. If you have any questions to ask, I would be happy to answer. Thank you all so much for listening.

Organization and HR

[TOC]

Organizational charts

Organizing

is a process of arranging and structuring work to accomplish organizational goals

Organizational structure

is how work is divided, supervised and coordinated.

Tasks

The major activities of the organization

Subdivisions

Which departments are responsible for which tasks

Levels

The position of each post within the hierarchy

Lines of authority

Links—show who people report to

6 Elements of structure design

Work specialization

  • Horizontal specialization

  • Dividing work activities into separate job tasks

Departmentalization

  • The basis by which jobs are grouped together

Chain of command

  • The line of authority extending from upper organizational levels to the lowest levels, which clarifies who reports to whom

The span of control

  • The number of employees a manager can efficiently and effectively manage

Centralization and Decentralization

  • Vertical specialization

  • The degree to which decision making is concentrated at upper levels of the organisation or the opposite (at lower levels)

Formalization

  • how standardized an organisation’s jobs are and the extent to which employee behavior is guided by rules and procedures.

Three types of structures

  • Based on horizontal specialization

Functional

  • Common professional or other expertise

  • when tasks are grouped into departments based on similar skills and expertise.

Pros cons
Employees are grouped with others who have similar tasks Managers lose sight of what’s best for the overall organization in pursuing functional goals
Cost-saving (economies of scale, minimal duplication of people/equipment) Functional specialists become insulated and have little understanding of what other units are doing

Divisional

  • Products, customers or geography

  • when tasks are grouped in relation to their outputs, such as products or the needs of different types of customer.

    | Pros | Cons |
    | :———————————————————-: | :———————————————————-: |
    | focuses on results: division managers are responsible for what happens to their products and services | Costs increases and efficiency reduces due to duplication of activities and resources. |

Matrix

  • In functional groups, work on division tasks

  • when those doing a task report both to a functional and a project or divisional boss

Pros Cons
Balance the benefits of functional and divisional forms Can lead to conflicts over resources
What do you do if your two bosses disagree
Duplication of activity

Roles of HR managers

Human resource management(HRM)

  • Formal systems devised for the management of people within an organization

3 major areas

  • Staffing
    • recruiting and selection
  • Employee compensation and benefits
  • defining/ designing work

WHY HRM important?

Theoretical model of the effects of HPWS on organizational outcomes

HR process

HR Planning Job analysis Recruiting and Selection Reward management

HR planning

  • ensuring that the organization has the right number and kinds of capable people in the right places and at the right time.
    • Assessing current human resource
    • Meeting future HR needs.
  • Limitations of forecasting techniques encourage managers to create more flexibility as an alternative way to balance supply and demand

Job analysis and job description

Analysis

  • the process of determining the characteristics of an area of work according to a prescribed set of dimensions.Issues to consider include:
    • Collect data for the job description
    • Decide who should collect this information
    • Decide how the job information should be structured and put into a standard format

Job description

  • A written statement outlining the duties and responsibilities associated with a given job.

Recruitment and selection process

Recruitment

  • Aims to produce a good pool of applicants
  • Recruiting sources:
    • Internet
    • Employee referrals(员工介绍)
    • University fairs
    • Company website
    • Professional recruiting organizations

Selection

  • Managers use methods they hope will minimize:

    • False-positive errors: hire the wrong people
    • False-negative errors: reject the right people

  • Validity occurs when there is a statistically significant relationship between a predictor (such as a selection test score) and measures of on-the-job performance
